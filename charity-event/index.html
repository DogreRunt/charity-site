Bobby Black 5k for Amyloidosis


About Amyloidosis

Bobby Black will forever be loved by his community. After his amyloidosis diagnosis, against all odds, he was blessed to have 8 additional years to shine, to smile, to make a difference. Amyloidosis is a rare disease, affecting less than 200,000 Americans with about 4,500 new cases each year.

Amyloidosis is caused by a bone marrow disorder. The bone marrow in the center of bones produces cells in the blood system. These cells are the part of the immune system that makes antibodies for fighting infections. Proteins are also produced by the cells in the bone marrow. In Amyloidosis patients, plasma cells produce an abnormal antibody protein. The misfolded amyloid proteins are deposited in and around tissues, nerves and organs. As the amyloid builds up in an organ, nerve or tissue, it gradually causes damage and affects their function. Each amyloidosis patient has a different pattern of amyloid deposition in their body. It often affects more than one organ and can last from several years to a lifetime and sadly, it took the life of our beloved teacher Mr. Black.

About the Event

On Saturday, June 17, 2017 at Conestee Park in Greenville, SC we will honor Bobby's memory with a 5k race. All funds will be donated to the Amyloidosis Foundation.

To honor his legacy, his friends and family want to give back by hosting this 5k event to support amyloidosis research. With our donations, we will help others find a cure. Please help raise money for the Amyloidosis Foundation and help us to help others.
